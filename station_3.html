Instructions

The game will consist of three different stages, simulating different important aspects of entrepreneurship. Said stages will include earning capital, human resource management and asset management.

The teams will be given a passbook to keep records of their capital remaining and decisions in each stage. The game will consist of three different stages:

     

  1. Capital Earning

Duration: 5 minutes

The teams will begin the game by first earning some capital. This is similar to reality, where before any business can begin doing anything (such as hiring employees, purchasing assets and such), it needs to attain some capital and gather resources.

The group will be given a long string, and a list of items which they can gather. The team will then have to gather this list of items and tie them onto the string. They will be given 3 minutes to gather as many as they can and present it to the game master.

The amount of capital awarded will range from $25,000 to $50,000, following the table below:

After they have earned their capital, the game master will brief the teams on how this capital will come into play for the rest of the game.

For the rest of the game, the teams will have to spend their capital on several activities, which will bring about different rewards depending on their decisions. The game will simulate their first year, where their different investments will only bring about rewards at the end of the game (i.e. at the end of the first year).

 

  1. Hiring Workers

Duration: 4 minutes

The groups will have to choose to hire the employee(s) of their choice or may opt not to hire as well. Should they choose to hire an employee, they will have to pay the specified wage to the station helper designated. The wage and description per employee will be disclosed, but not the profit that will be added to the end.

This station emphasizes the importance of human resources, and hiring good employees, by awarding profits for any employee hired, with greater wage producing a greater margin of profits.

 

  1. Purchasing assets

Duration: 5-6 minutes

In this stage, teams will have to play a game to be given opportunities to purchase the different assets. List of assets will be disclosed in the game.

The teams will have a set time limit of 30 seconds to bounce ping pong balls into plastic cups. The cups will be labelled accordingly (per asset choice), and if the team manages to bounce the ball into the cup, they will be given the option to purchase the asset (i.e. they can choose not to purchase it).

Some assets will result in greater profits, some will even result in losses.
